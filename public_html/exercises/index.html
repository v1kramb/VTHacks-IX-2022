This page covers the two exercises that the app critiques, pushups and squats, specifically detailing the optimal form for these exercises.

Pushup

The pushup is a common bodyweight exercise that builds strength by raising and lowering the body while keeping a straight back.

A pushup is done by getting down on all fours, straighening your arms and legs, lowering your body until your chest almost touches the floor, and then pushing yourself back up.

The three most important parts of a pushup are keeping a straight back, keeping your arms bent at a 90° angle, and making sure that your chest does not touch the floor.

 

Squat

The squat is an exercise that targets the muscles in the lower legs, lower back, and core. It is considered a dynamic strength training exercise because it works several muscles in a person's lower and upper body simultaniously.

A squatt is done by standing with the feet shoulder-width apart and the toes pointing slightly outward, bending the knees to push the hips backwards, and straitening the legs once the knees reach an angle of 90° or lower.

The keys to having good squat form are keeping your knees in line with your feet, putting your weight on the balls of your feet, and keeping your heels on the floor throughout the squat.
